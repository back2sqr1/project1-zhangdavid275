WHAT IS LAIN

IPv7 - Layer 01 | Who are you? | portfolio

Serial Experiment Lain is a Japanese anime series that explores themes of identity, reality, and technology. It delves into the blurred lines between the physical world and the virtual realm known as "The Wired," where consciousness can be transmitted and altered. The show follows Lain Iwakura, a teenage girl who becomes increasingly immersed in this digital landscape, questioning her own existence and the nature of reality.

In terms of aesthetic, David finds himself obsessed with the cyberpunk and glitch art style of the series, which often features distorted visuals, neon colors, and a dark, moody atmosphere. This aesthetic reflects the show's themes of technology and its impact on society, as well as the blurred lines between reality and the virtual world. The use of glitch art in particular emphasizes the idea of fragmentation and disconnection, which is a recurring theme throughout the series. It truly is one of the biggest reasons why David loves programming and computing in general. He wonders who sort of life he would have lived if he didn't watch this show.
